Executive Summary

  • The problem: Leadership burns 15+ minutes each week stitching together writer output, production throughput, and scale-readiness signals across four different tabs — decisions that should take 60 seconds are delayed by fragmented tooling.
  • The investment: Two targeted capability additions — a Content Ops Command Center (Overview Tab) and an Editorial Velocity Engine (POD-level throughput) — that unify the three questions every content leader asks on Monday morning: How much did we ship? How fast did production move? What's ready to scale?
  • The expected return: Reduce weekly leadership review time by 80%, surface underperforming PODs before the week closes, and shorten the content-to-scale decision cycle by bringing production and analytics signals into the same line of sight as editorial output.

Strategic Goals

Why Now

The editorial operation has scaled to the point where individual-tab navigation creates a measurable drag on decision speed. POD leads lack a self-service benchmark to course-correct mid-week. Production output and scale-readiness signals live in separate views with no connective tissue. These are not feature gaps — they are operational intelligence gaps that compound every week they remain unfilled.

Desired Outcomes

60-Second Leadership Briefing

A VP of Editorial opens one tab and knows: total fresh takes shipped, which POD led, how production tracked, and what's ready to scale. No tab-switching. No mental math.

Self-Service POD Benchmarking

POD leads see where they rank against peers on throughput %, enabling mid-week intervention without waiting for a weekly review.

Scale-Readiness at a Glance

Assets meeting Full Gen AI criteria are surfaced with actioned state — leadership sees what's been greenlit and what's pending decision in one view.

Zero New Data Entry

Every metric derives from existing Google Sheets and Supabase sources. No new manual workflows, no additional data burden on creators or producers.

NEW CAPABILITIES

1. Content Ops Command Center (Overview Tab) New

A single-pane-of-glass that mirrors every other tab at a summary level — giving leadership the full pipeline picture in one scroll. Four subsections map to the four operational questions: Are we committing enough beats? Are writers delivering? Is production keeping pace? Are ads performing?

◆
Strategic rationale: The Overview tab is a read-only intelligence layer that pulls the most critical signals from Editorial, Production, and Analytics into a unified weekly view. All editing and drill-downs remain in their respective source tabs. Week selector defaults to last completed week.
Subsection 1: Beats New

Business Question

Are we making enough assets to cover the committed beat plan — and where are the gaps?

Metrics

MetricDefinitionDisplay
Beats to be madeTotal beats committed/planned for the selected week across all PODsLarge hero number
Beats madeBeats where ≥1 fresh take reached Ready for Prod or beyondLarge hero number
Assets madeTotal assets (fresh takes) produced against committed beatsLarge hero number
Assets away from beatsGap: Beats committed minus assets made. Any shortfall.Red highlight when > 0

Experience Design

  • Four hero numbers in a row — beats to be made, beats made, assets made, and the gap number
  • The gap number (assets away from beats) renders in red when there is any shortfall — an immediate visual signal that the pipeline is under-delivering
  • When gap = 0 or assets exceed beats, the number renders in green

Data Sources

  • Planner → committed beat set for the week (beats to be made)
  • Editorial writer-tracker → beats delivered + assets produced
  • Gap is computed client-side: beatsToMake - assetsMade

2. Editorial Velocity Engine — POD-level Throughput Enhancement

Transforms the Editorial Funnel from a writer-level tracking tool into a competitive intelligence surface for editorial leadership. Adds a POD-level throughput leaderboard that answers the question every editorial lead asks: which POD is outperforming, and who needs intervention?

⚠
Scope note: This is an additive enhancement to existing Editorial sub-views. All existing writer-level detail is preserved. The POD-level leaderboard is injected as the first section in "Overview" and "Last Week" sub-views — giving leadership the macro signal before the micro detail.
POD Throughput Leaderboard Enhancement — Editorial Overview + Last Week

Business Question

Which POD has the highest creative throughput this week, and how does each POD's conversion rate compare?

Throughput Metric

  • Beats committed — total beats the POD was assigned for the week (the denominator of pipeline efficiency)
  • Beats delivered — beats where ≥1 fresh take reached Ready for Prod or beyond by week end
  • Throughput % = Beats delivered / Beats committed × 100 (the single number that defines POD velocity)
  • Rank — sorted descending by Throughput %, ties broken by absolute count delivered

Experience Design — Leaderboard

  • Ranked card row for each POD: rank badge (gold/silver/bronze for top 3), POD lead name, Throughput %, mini progress bar (delivered vs. committed), absolute numbers
  • Click-through → scrolls to writer-level detail for that POD in "This Week" sub-view (seamless drill-down)
  • Responsive: stacks to vertical list on mobile

POD Summary Card Metrics

  • Beats committed this week
  • Beats delivered (reached Ready for Prod)
  • Throughput % — large, colour-coded: green ≥80%, amber 60–79%, red <60%
  • Writers in POD (team size context)
  • Average output per writer (talent efficiency signal)

Writer-level Enhancements

  • POD throughput summary header above each POD's writer group in "This Week"
  • POD rank badge next to POD lead name
  • "Top performer" highlight (most delivered) per POD with a subtle accent — recognizes high-velocity contributors

Data Sources

  • Existing /api/dashboard/writer-tracker — writer-level stage data grouped by POD
  • Existing /api/dashboard/overview?period=last|current — beat counts and stage distributions
  • Add pods[] with pre-computed throughput + rank to writer-tracker API response

Planner Launched

The resource allocation engine — an interactive Gantt board for week-level scripting planning that drives the commitment numbers every downstream metric depends on.

Brush-paint Gantt board

Click-drag to paint beats/stages across days and writers. Stage pipeline: Writing → Pending Review → Reviewed → Ready for Prod.

Roster manager

Add/remove/archive writers per POD, move writers between PODs, edit names and roles. Talent utilization starts here.

Beat management

Add beats to writers, fetch beat docs from Google Sheets, remove beats, beat picker search.

Week navigation & commit

Navigate previous/next weeks. Commit "next week" snapshot which drives Editorial Next Week metrics.

Persistence

Auto-saves to Supabase bucket (weeks/{weekKey}.json). Edit mode locking with auth.

Share

Copy planner as image to clipboard. Share link with snapshot for async review.

Risks & Dependencies

RiskSeverityMitigation
"Approved beat" definition is ambiguous — different stakeholders may define the committed beat set differently (Ready for Production vs. Ideation commits) High Resolve with Editorial Lead before Phase 1 build begins. Document definition in Details tab.
Actioned state adoption — Gen AI scale numbers are only meaningful if POD leads consistently mark assets as actioned Medium Surface "pending decision" count prominently to create social pressure for timely actioning. Track actioned latency as a secondary metric.
ACD throughput unit ambiguity — "production output" could mean minutes, image count, or a composite Medium Align with Production Lead on unit before Phase 2. Default to existing production metric unit.
Google Sheets API rate limits — Overview Tab adds incremental API calls on every load Low Overview Tab reuses cached data from existing endpoints. No net-new Sheets calls at initial load.
Week selector scope creep — Overview Tab week selector could diverge from tab-level selectors, confusing users Low Keep Overview Tab's week selector independent initially. Evaluate global week context in a future iteration.
